01 Due Diligence

What to check before any LNS inspection.

Most buyers treat due diligence as something that happens after they've decided they want a property. That's backwards. The buyers who move confidently here have already done the work before the first open home. Here's what that looks like in practice.

Confirms no outstanding orders against the lot. Required for LNS apartments given heritage overlay prevalence in inner-LNS suburbs.

The due diligence ran after they identified a property they wanted. Which means it ran under pressure, with a timeline the selling agent controls. Doing it in advance changes the dynamic entirely.

02 Market Access

Off-market and pre-market.
What the terms actually mean.

Both terms get used loosely enough on the LNS that they've stopped meaning much to most buyers. The distinction matters because it changes what preparation is required and what access is even possible.

Public Campaign

Listed on Domain and realestate.com.au. Open homes scheduled. Price guide published. Average of 30–40 competing buyers at first open home on LNS properties above $3M.

You're in the queue

Pre-Market

Property is coming to market but hasn't launched publicly. 7–14 day window. Positioned buyers and their agents get first access. Due diligence can be completed. An offer accepted before the open home queue ever forms.

Access = agent relationships

Genuine Off-Market

No campaign. No portal listing. No open homes. A vendor who wants a considered transaction without public exposure. The selling agent calls buyer's agents whose clients they know are serious. That call either comes or it doesn't.

Access = trust built in advance

Pre-market and off-market are not interchangeable. Pre-market is a timing advantage — you're early. Off-market is a relationship advantage — you're in a conversation most buyers don't know is happening. More than 80% of what BMONEY buys on the LNS is completed through one of these two channels. That figure isn't a marketing claim. It reflects how much of this market operates before a public campaign ever launches.

The pre-market window — how it opens and closes

Why this matters for you specifically: The LNS selling agent market is tight. The same 40–60 agents handle the majority of transactions above $3M across Mosman, Cremorne, Neutral Bay and Cammeray. When a vendor decides to move quietly, those agents call the buyer's agents whose clients they've transacted with, whose qualification they trust, whose word means something. Five years of exclusive LNS focus means those calls come to us.
